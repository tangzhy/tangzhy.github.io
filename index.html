About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-Shenzhen), fortunately advised by Prof. Benyou Wang and co-supervised by Ming Yan. I've had the privilege of working with amazing teams at Tencent Hunyuan Team, Kimi Team, Qwen Team, and MSRA GenAI Group.

Research: My current research centers on agentic systems that can perceive, reason, and act in real environments. I am especially interested in coding agents, computer-use agents, and phone-use agents, with an emphasis on making them more capable, reliable, and open.

On the capability side, I study tool-integrated reasoning, agent mid-training, and reinforcement learning (RL) for complex multi-step tasks, as reflected in works such as CoRT, STORM, and SCRIT. On the evaluation side, I care about benchmarks and auditing frameworks that measure not only task success, but also privacy, reliability, and generalization, including my recent work MyPhoneBench. More broadly, I am interested in open and reproducible approaches that help break the wall of closed-source distillation.

I am actively seeking full-time research or engineering roles starting around July 2026. I also welcome students interested in these topics; feel free to send your CV to my email.
